Body Mass Index scores or BMI scores indicate whether a person's weight is in a healthy range for their height, but it's essential to interpret the results with caution. A BMI score categorises individuals into underweight (less than 18.5), normal weight (18.5-24.9), overweight (25-29.9), and obese (30 or higher). While BMI doesn't directly measure body fat or health, research suggests that being in the normal weight range is associated with lower risks of chronic diseases, such as heart disease, diabetes, and certain cancers. If your BMI is low, consider consulting a healthcare professional or registered dietitian to develop a weight gain plan focusing on nutrient-dense foods. Conversely, if your BMI is high, aim to lose weight through sustainable lifestyle changes, including a balanced diet and regular physical activity. The World Health Organization recommends adults strive for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise per week.
